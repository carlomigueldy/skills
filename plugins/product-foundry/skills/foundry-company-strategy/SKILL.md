# Company strategy phase

Translate evidence into falsifiable commercial decisions. Read
`../../references/workflow.md` for state and approval behavior,
`../../references/policies.md` before high-consequence decisions or delegation,
and `../../references/platform-adapter.md` and
`../../references/artifact-contract.md` before presenting choices or creating
artifacts.

## Required inputs

Require approved intake and research evidence, including unresolved
assumptions. For a direct revision, create a new draft but do not unlock MVP
definition until the revised strategy is approved.

## Run the phase

1. Verify approved intake and research; list unresolved assumptions and
   contradictory evidence that constrain the recommendation.
2. Offer exactly `Interview me` and `Decide for me` for this phase; do not
   inherit a prior choice. Ask one material question at a time and use stable
   platform-adapter options when choices are presented.
3. Define the ICP and segments, customer job, company and product thesis,
   differentiation, pricing and packaging hypotheses, channel or operating
   constraints, and relevant legal or privacy assumptions.
4. Define measurable activation, retention, revenue, and qualitative PMF
   signals, including explicit pivot, persevere, and stop criteria. Label
   evidence, inference, and assumption; do not manufacture precision.
5. Write the strategy, move it from `draft` to `in_review`, and offer approval,
   changes, or a return to research. Do not infer approval from silence.

## Deterministic outputs

Write `docs/company/strategy.md` with the commercial hypothesis, decision
criteria, evidence links, and open risks. Consolidate small products instead of
creating empty companion files.

## Completion criteria

Make the commercial hypothesis, decision criteria, evidence boundaries, and
open risks explicit and ready for review.

## Invalidation rules

A changed customer,
evidence base, pricing assumption, operating constraint, or success criterion
marks affected MVP, marketing, and downstream plans stale; revisit only the
affected decisions and their dependencies.

## Participation gate

Offer `Interview me` and `Decide for me`, selected independently at the start of every phase; do not inherit a prior choice. In recommendation mode, record
rationale, evidence, assumptions, confidence, risks, revisit conditions, and
user authority. Do not resolve high-consequence decisions for the user.

## Approval gate

Require explicit approval before unlocking MVP definition. Offer approval,
changes, or a return to research; never infer approval from silence.

## Safety and delegation

Keep recommendations reversible and distinguish planning from execution.
Require explicit approval before legal acceptance, paid pricing tests, external
commitments, accounts, domains, or production actions. Delegate bounded
analysis only under the shared policy, record escalations, retain one canonical
writer, and review delegated conclusions before using them in the strategy.
